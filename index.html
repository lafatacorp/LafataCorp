003 -- About

Cam Lafata

Cam Lafata is a sports media professional and entrepreneur based in Dallas-Fort Worth. The only member of his family who's an actual Texas native, Cam was born in Dallas and spent his early childhood in Frisco before moving to Boston, Massachusetts at the age of ten. He spent most of his formative years in New England, where his family is from, before making the decision to return to his hometown. The move would eventually lead him to Southern Methodist University, where he studies Sport Management through the Simmons School of Education and Human Development alongside business at the Cox School of Business, with concentrated work in sports law and ethics, sports data analytics, organizational leadership, and communications.

Lafata currently serves as an on-air personality at KTCK 96.7/1310 The Ticket -- one of the most listened-to sports radio stations in the country and a cornerstone of DFW's sports media landscape -- where he contributes live broadcast coverage, editorial commentary, and sports analysis across the NFL, NBA, MLB, and NCAA, with a particular focus on the NHL. That focus is intentional: Lafata has a long-term ambition to work in hockey operations at the professional level, bringing an analytical, communication-driven approach to the front office side of the game he knows best.

A senior at SMU, Lafata has built a career at the intersection of media, analytics, and emerging markets. His academic research explores the behavioral and financial impact of legalized sports betting on young adults, alongside the growing role of prediction markets as alternative investment vehicles and specifically, the opportunity this asset class presents to a younger generation of investors. It is work that bridges behavioral finance theory with real-world market dynamics.

Beyond the microphone, Lafata is the founder of LafataCorp, a venture focused on predictive market intelligence. The company develops proprietary algorithmic systems that identify edge across event-driven markets through statistical modeling, sentiment analysis, and automated execution. What began as an independent research project has evolved into an operational trading infrastructure with a disciplined, data-first approach to risk-managed returns.
